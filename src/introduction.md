# Introduction

## What is this book?

This book is a **very** opinionated guide on course registration as a **Computer Science, B.Sc., Major** *or* **Computer Science, B.C.Sc., Honours**, *with* or *without* Co-op option, undergraduate at the University of Manitoba.

## Who is this book for?

Incoming students of the University of Manitoba, current students of any seniority at the University of Manitoba, whether they are in Computer Science or not; though, of course, you would mainly benefit if you are doing, or are intending to do, Computer Science.

## Why does this book exist?

Because no guide at this level of detail currently exists, because the author has seen too many peers make subpar course registration decisions they later regret, and because the author had a sudden flash of inspiration come at them one random Tuesday midnight.

## How is this book structured?

The book begins with [a chapter on things the author expect the reader to have consulted before continuing with this book](./prerequisites.md). Afterwards, the book is broken down into different credit hour milestones, rather than distinction by year of study. The reasoning for this is because, even though the program is designed to be completed in four years, the author would pay you $2 if you can verify a single individual actually completed their program in four years the "intended way" (as described in the Academic Calendar). While distinction via credit hours can be confusing as it is unconventional, the author believes there are enough people with "unconventional" degree paths that breaking down different milestones in terms of credit hours would result in a more digestible reading experience.

Each milestone contains, for predictability's sake, the following sections:

* Why that specific number was chosen as a milestone
* Requirements to "graduate" from the milestone
* TL;DR course recommendations and when to take them
* How to pick "options" course (more on this later)

## When was this book last updated?

The book was last updated as of **June 23th, 2026**, after the release of the 2026-2027 Preview Academic Calendar. For older readers, this course thus assumes the normalization of the new second- and third-year courses, as well as remove consideration of the courses they are intended to replace. The book will probably be updated a year from now at minimum by the original author, and this section can be updated as other individuals contribute to it.

## Contributing

Despite the opinionated nature, the guide is fully open to contribution. [Open an issue on GitHub](https://github.com/black-witch-forest/cs-course-registration/issues), discuss it with the repository owner ([me](https://github.com/pieberrykinnie)), and if I approve of the suggestion, either open a pull request with content edits yourself or I will also probably get to it.

The guide is built on top of [mdBook](https://rust-lang.github.io/mdBook/), a lightweight framework for generating static pages off of Markdown files built using Rust. Installation is super easy if you want to test changes locally, and it is hopefully obvious how the entire thing works, but feel free to contact me via GitHub Issues if you need any technical assistance.
