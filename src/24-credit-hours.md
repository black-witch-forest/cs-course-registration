# 24 Credit Hours

## Why is this a milestone?

You require completion of 24 credit hours before you can declare a program in either the Major or Honours, which should be your only goal at this time in your studies (aside from boosting your GPA).

## Requirements

To complete this milestone, you will have completed 24 credit hours, of which must include:

* COMP 1010 (or COMP 1012) with a C
  * If you have Computer Science 40S credit from high school, this is not required and can be substituted with any elective.
* COMP 1020 with a C+ (Major) or B (Honours)
* MATH 1240 with a C+ (Major) or B (Honours)
* MATH 1500 (or MATH 1230 or MATH 1510) with a C
* MATH 1700 (or MATH 1232 or MATH 1710) with a C
* MATH 1300 (or MATH 1220 or MATH 1210 (B)) with a C+
* STAT 1150 (or STAT 1000 *and* STAT 2000 (B)) with a C

## Planning

Note that, if you have eligible AP or IB credits, you can probably [transfer some credits](https://umanitoba.ca/explore/undergraduate-admissions/requirements/transfer-credit-information) and save yourself the trouble. Aside from electives, you can also potentially get credits directly for COMP 1010, COMP 1020, MATH 1500, and MATH 1700, lightening your workload.

All these courses are offered year-round, so you have a lot of flexibility. Some things to get out of the way:

* It is 99.99% in your best interest to take COMP 1010, MATH 1500, MATH 1700, MATH 1300, and STAT 1150 over their substitutes. Only consider their substitutes if and **only** if:
  * The waitlists are huge (40+ students) and you do not feel like risking it (although you should still wait until the very last day of add date to jump ships)
  * You are *very* experienced at programming in Java (you are aware of all of primitives and objects, arrays, recursion, and inheritance at the minimum) and you want to learn Python for the lolz, take COMP 1012
  * You want the flexibility of switching to another degree that accepts only MATH 1210 (like Engineering degrees)
* If there is one course you absolutely must NOT substitute, it is STAT 1150. There is never a reason to take STAT 1000 and 2000 over 1150.
* Even if you have Computer Science 40S, it probably is in your best interest to take COMP 1010. The only time it is not is when you should probably be taking COMP 1012.
* Your utmost priority in your earliest credit hours is to **boost your GPA as much as humanly possible**.

### Dependencies

Some courses require others to be completed:

* COMP 1020 requires COMP 1010 (or 1012)
* MATH 1700 (or its substitutes) requires MATH 1500 (or its substitutes)
* MATH 1300, MATH 1240, and STAT 1150 do not depend on anything

### Guiding Principles

* Get COMP 1010 and MATH 1500 out of the way ASAP
* Get COMP 1020 out of the way ASAP after the two above
* MATH 1240 is historically the "hardest first-year course", but after significant content reduction, MATH 1700 is arguably harder
* STAT 1150 has some programming and thus should be done after COMP 1010 if you had no prior coding experience
* COMP 1010, MATH 1240, MATH 1500, and STAT 1150 are *historically* available in the May-June summer term, while COMP 1020, MATH 1300, and MATH 1700 are *historically* available in the July-August summer term

### Sample Registration Timelines

#### Conventional Route

* Fall: COMP 1010, MATH 1500, MATH 1300, Elective
* Winter: COMP 1020, MATH 1700, MATH 1240, STAT 1150

You can alternately push MATH 1240, STAT 1150, or the elective to the May-June term, **NOT** July-August. As then, you will not have all requirements completed before course registration opens.

#### Starting Winter

* Winter: COMP 1010, MATH 1500, Elective
* Summer: Any (optional)
* Fall: COMP 1020, MATH 1700, STAT 1150
* Winter: MATH 1240, MATH 1300, Elective

#### Steady Onboarding

* Fall: COMP 1000, MATH 1500, Elective
* Winter: COMP 1010, MATH 1700, Elective
* Summer: Any (optional)
* Fall: COMP 1020, MATH 1300, Elective
* Winter: MATH 1240, STAT 1150, Elective

In general, every schedule above accomplishes one thing: **ensuring you have all requirements completed before a July registration window, can declare your program, and start your second year "conventionally" regardless of how long you take to complete the milestone**.

## Course Options

For the electives, your best bet is currently anything recommended in the "Recommended Introductory Courses" mentioned in the FYPG.

The first year COMP electives are also interesting: you can read more about them in the [official description from the Department](https://umanitoba.ca/science/computer-science/course-offerings#a-guide-to-first-year-computer-science-courses). If you had to pick one, I would recommend COMP 1500 if you want to get a broader introduction to Computer Science that is not Java Programming 101, or COMP 1002 + 1006 if you want to dig straight into the technical toolkits real developers actually use every day.

I would recommend against "optimizing" your first few electives to "benefit your degree": you are honestly better off trying other things, even outside of STEM, to pursue your interdisciplinary interests and perhaps even get a Minor. But if you are that kind of person, as mentioned above, the COMP electives are your best bet; taking second-year MATH and/or STAT courses are also beneficial if you intend to go into math-heavy fields like machine learning or quantitative finance.
